Adventures In Photography

I have been consistently working with photography for the past 6 years, Independently and for businesses. I have been working for The Brock Press for the past year and a half and I have noticed significant improvement in my skills. I have also been exposed to a wider range of photography that I previously didn't have experience with, sports in particular. In the past I mainly photographed bands but I more recently have a growing appreciation for sport photography. Of everything I have done it requires the most concentration since everything happens so fast. Portrait photography is something I am still working on, I have never been a fan of super posed studio photography. My goal with portraits is for them to have a natural look and capture the subjects personality.

I recently picked up a Polaroid Instant camera, it's a lot of fun and I highly recommend it to anyone who enjoys photography. It forces me to think about my photo before I take it, with only 8 exposures per roll there is no room for error (especially with the price of film). Mostly it's a lot of fun to have a physical photo instantly, there is also a certain quality film has to it which is difficult to replicate with a digital camera giving it a unique look.

Top 5 Photography Must Haves

  1. An 18-135 lens
  2. 50mm (prime) lens
  3. A decent camera bag (I recomend the backpack kind)
  4. Tripod
  5. 70-200 lens

Above are a few pieces of gear that I highly recommend having if you are photographing a wide variety of thing like me. I also recommend a flash but didn't include it in my list because I far prefer using natural light and where there is none the 50 Prime lens will take care of you. The majority of my music photography was taken with one.
